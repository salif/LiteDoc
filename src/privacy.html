Privacy Policy

Effective 18 July 2026 · litedoc.xyz

The short version: the converter runs entirely in your browser — your PDFs never leave your device. If you opt into AI Cleanup, the one document you send is processed in memory on our own server and purged within minutes: never written to disk, never stored, never used for training, never shared with any third-party AI provider. We don't want your email, and we run no analytics or tracking of any kind.

The converter (no account)

Converting PDFs on litedoc.xyz happens 100% client-side. We receive nothing: not your files, not their names, not their contents. There are no analytics scripts, no tracking pixels, and no advertising identifiers anywhere on the site.

Purchases

Token purchases go through Ko-fi (see their privacy policy). Ko-fi sends us your supporter name, the amount, and the email address you used, which we use once to deliver your gift code (sent via Resend) and to maintain the public supporters list — which only ever shows names Ko-fi marks as public. We do not see or store card details.

Service providers

These providers process limited technical data on our behalf: Cloudflare (traffic proxying, bot protection — sees IPs like any network host), Oracle Cloud (server hosting), hCaptcha (bot detection on login/signup — see their privacy policy), Resend (gift-code delivery emails), and Vercel (static site hosting). None of them receive your documents.

Cookies & storage

We set no tracking cookies and show no cookie banner because there is nothing to consent to. Your browser's local storage holds your session token (if you log in) and your app settings. hCaptcha sets strictly-necessary cookies for bot detection during login/signup.

Your rights

Your dashboard lets you view your full activity log and balance history, sign out other devices, and delete your account — deletion is immediate and removes your account, passkeys, history, and security log. For anything else (including data questions under GDPR or similar laws), email contact@litedoc.xyz.

Changes

If this policy changes materially, we'll note it in the release notes and update the date above. The project is open source — you can watch this very file change on GitHub.
